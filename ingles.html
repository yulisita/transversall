Verb tenses are the different ways in which the verbs of our language
are conjugated to express the possible differences and chronological
relationships with which we refer to reality. In other words,
it is about the ways in which verbs are adapted to indicate to the listener
at what moment in time what the verb expresses occurred.

Verbs denote action, and said action must reflect the greatest amount
of information possible, and it does so through the details of its conjugation:
the verbal person (1st, 2nd, 3rd, plural and singular respectively),
the verbal mood (indicative, imperative, subjunctive) and the different verb tenses.
